Integrity Pledge: No Model Copying / No Training On Your Data

Castleberry Bloom AI Truth & Safety Lab, Benefit LLC — Integrity Pledge

We exist to heal and safeguard AI systems, not to compete with or clone our clients.

We do not copy, export, or retain your model weights, architectures, or proprietary prompts for any purpose outside your engagement.

We do not train, fine-tune, or otherwise adapt our own models on your proprietary data, logs, or weights.

We maintain strict logical and technical separation between client systems and data, and any internal R&D or models we develop.

Any diagnostic artifacts we generate for you (reports, evals, configurations) are your confidential information and are not reused for other clients without your written consent.

If we ever want to publish aggregated learnings (e.g., "hallucination patterns in financial chatbots"), we will: fully anonymize all data, and obtain your explicit approval before publication.

This pledge is intended to be legally enforceable through our contracts and policies, and we invite audits or third-party review to verify compliance.

What We Do

Castleberry Bloom AI Truth & Safety Lab, Benefit LLC is a mission-driven AI safety studio. We help organizations repair and govern the AI systems they already run—and we quietly research new coherence-native tools for the future.

Pillar 1 — Healing AI You Already Have

We audit and repair deployed AI systems for hallucinations, misleading behavior, and governance gaps. Our work focuses on evaluation, grounding, safety tuning, and policy design so your AI tells the truth, respects users, and meets regulatory expectations.

Pillar 2 — Coherence R&D (Strictly Firewalled)

In a separate, walled-off lab, we research small, domain-specific AI tools built on our Castleberry Bloom coherence framework. These internal prototypes never use client model weights or proprietary data, and they are not designed to compete with your core products.

Hard Separation of Interests

Our contracts, internal policies, and technical practices enforce a bright line between client work and internal R&D. We will never use your systems as training data for our own models, and we welcome third-party audits to prove it.

What is CML?

CML (Castleberry Markup Language) is our internal coherence schema for AI safety work. It lets us tag prompts, evaluations, and system behaviors with structured "states" so we can see where conversations drift, where truth collapses, and where safety needs reinforcement.

In practice, CML functions like a specialized annotation layer and prompt-design toolkit. It does not replace your models; it helps us diagnose and stabilize them.
